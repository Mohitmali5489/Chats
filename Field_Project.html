‚úÖ Step-by-Step Guide to Prepare Your Field Project:

1. Choose Your Topic
Select a realistic, socially relevant, and manageable topic. Take suggestions from your mentor.

2. Get Approval and Allotment
Submit your topic proposal for approval. You will be allotted a Faculty Mentor to guide you.

3. Plan Your Field Work
Choose location(s) and target groups. Schedule 2-3 field visits and prepare your survey tools.

4. Conduct the Field Work
Visit the selected sites and collect data via surveys, interviews, and observations. Always take consent and maintain privacy.

5. Maintain Records
Fill the Guide Interaction Diary (at least 5 meetings) and record your observations and feedback.

6. Organize Your Report Structure

  • Use Times New Roman, 12pt, 1.5 spacing.
  • Report should have:
    1. Title Page
    2. Certificate
    3. Declaration
    4. Acknowledgment
    5. Abstract
    6. Table of Contents
    7. Chapters:
      • Introduction
      • Literature Review
      • Methodology
      • Observations & Analysis
      • Conclusion & Recommendations
    8. References
    9. Appendices

7. Submit Documents
Submit the report along with the Guide Diary, Declaration, Certificate, and Feedback Form.

8. Presentation & Evaluation
Be ready for your presentation (viva). Evaluation is based on fieldwork, report quality, and presentation skills.
